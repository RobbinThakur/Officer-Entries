About NDA Entry

The National Defence Academy (NDA) is a tri-service academy that provides undergraduate training to prospective officers of the Indian Armed Forces. Located in Khadakwasla near Pune, Maharashtra, NDA is the first tri-service academy in the world. NDA's main goal is to create leaders who are equipped with character, intellect, and professional knowledge to serve the country in peace and war.

The NDA entrance exam is held twice a year, and candidates who clear the exam are called for the SSB (Services Selection Board) interview. The selection process is rigorous and tests the candidate's mental and physical abilities. Successful candidates undergo a three-year training program at NDA before being commissioned as officers in the Indian Armed Forces.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age - Candidates must be between 16.5 and 19.5 years old.
  • Educational Qualification - Candidates must have passed the 10+2 or equivalent exam from a recognized board with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as subjects.
  • Physical Standards - Candidates must meet the minimum physical standards set by the NDA.

Selection Process

The selection process for NDA entry involves two stages:

  • Written Exam - The written exam consists of two papers - Mathematics and General Ability Test. Candidates who clear the written exam are called for the SSB interview.
  • SSB Interview - The SSB interview is a five-day long process that tests the candidate's mental and physical abilities. The interview consists of various tests, including the Psychological Test, Group Testing Officer Tasks, and Personal Interview.
